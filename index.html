Divs vs. Semantic Tags

It's important to understand when to use a generic `<div>` and when to use a semantic tag like `<section>` or `<article>`.

This is a <div>. It has no semantic meaning. It's just a "division" or a box. You use it for styling purposes, like creating a container for a group of elements that don't logically form a distinct section of the document.

This is an <article>

An `<article>` is for self-contained content that could be distributed on its own, like a blog post, a news story, or a forum comment. It makes sense by itself.

This whole area with the white background is a `<section>` tag. It groups related content together. Unlike an article, a section might not make sense if you took it out of the context of the page.
